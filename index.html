BTS SIO

What is the BTS SIO ?

The BTS SIO (Brevet de Technicien Supérieur Services Informatiques aux Organisations) is a two-year higher education diploma recognized by the French State (RNCP level 5). It trains students to become IT professionals capable of managing and developing information systems within organizations such as companies, public administrations or digital service companies.

During the training, students learn how to design, deploy and maintain IT solutions adapted to the needs of businesses. The program combines theoretical knowledge and practical projects in areas such as system administration, networking, software development, databases and cybersecurity.

The BTS SIO prepares students for immediate employment in the IT sector or for further studies such as a professional bachelor’s degree, engineering school or specialized IT training.

Option SLAM

SLAM stands for "Solutions Logicielles et Applications Métiers". This specialization focuses on software development and application design.

Students learn how to design, develop, test and maintain applications used by organizations. The training includes programming languages, web development, database management and application security.

Graduates from the SLAM option often work as web developers, application developers or software analysts. Their main mission is to create and maintain digital tools that support the activities of companies and users.

Option SISR

SISR means "Solutions d’Infrastructure, Systèmes et Réseaux". This specialization focuses on the installation, administration and security of IT infrastructures.

Students learn how to configure networks, manage servers, supervise IT systems and ensure the availability and security of digital services within organizations.

The SISR option prepares students for careers such as system administrator, network technician or IT infrastructure specialist responsible for maintaining and securing the company’s information system.

BTS SIO - Exams

The BTS SIO – SISR is evaluated through a combination of continuous assessments and final exams at the end of the two-year program. The final exams are organized into several subjects, each testing specific technical and professional skills. In total, the exams represent approximately 40 hours of written and practical evaluations, complemented by project presentations.

Detailed Exam Breakdown

  • 1. IT Project Management (E4) – 4 hours written, coefficient 5

    Students are evaluated on their ability to manage IT projects, plan tasks, write documentation, and propose solutions according to client needs. The exam includes case studies, project planning exercises, and analysis of IT needs.

  • 2. Network and Systems Administration (E5) – 5 hours written/practical, coefficient 6

    This exam tests the student’s skills in configuring, maintaining, and troubleshooting IT infrastructures. Tasks include server administration, network configuration, security implementation, and incident resolution.

  • 3. Cybersecurity & IT Security (SISR specific) – 3 hours written/practical, coefficient 4

    Students demonstrate their knowledge of network security, firewalls, user access management, and system protection. The exam includes scenarios on detecting vulnerabilities and proposing solutions to secure IT infrastructures.

  • 4. Technical Support and IT Assistance (E6) – 3 hours practical, coefficient 3

    Students are assessed on their ability to provide technical support to users, resolve incidents, and document procedures. Tasks simulate real-world IT assistance situations in organizations.

  • 5. Software Development & Scripting (SISR optional) – 4 hours written/practical, coefficient 3

    Although SISR focuses on infrastructure, students are expected to have basic scripting skills to automate tasks, manage system configurations, and support IT services.

  • 6. Professional Communication & Documentation (E7) – 2 hours written, coefficient 2

    This exam evaluates the ability to communicate effectively in a professional environment, write reports, and document projects and procedures clearly.

  • 7. Internship/Work Placement Evaluation (Période en entreprise) – Continuous assessment, coefficient 6

    Students’ internship performance is evaluated by supervisors and teachers. Criteria include technical proficiency, professional behavior, and the ability to apply theoretical knowledge to real-world situations.

Overall, the BTS SIO SISR exams combine practical IT skills, theoretical knowledge, and professional competencies to ensure that graduates are ready to manage and secure IT infrastructures in organizations.
