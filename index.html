BTS SIO

What is the BTS SIO?

The BTS SIO (Brevet de Technicien Supérieur Services Informatiques aux Organisations) is a two-year higher education diploma recognized by the French State (RNCP level 5). It trains students to become IT professionals capable of managing and developing information systems within organizations such as companies, public administrations, or digital service companies.

The program combines theoretical knowledge and practical projects in areas such as system administration, networking, software development, databases, and cybersecurity.

Option SLAM

SLAM stands for "Solutions Logicielles et Applications Métiers". This specialization focuses on software development and application design — programming languages, web development, database management, and application security.

Option SISR My Track

SISR means "Solutions d'Infrastructure, Systèmes et Réseaux". This specialization focuses on installing, administering, and securing IT infrastructures — configuring networks, managing servers, supervising IT systems, and ensuring security of digital services.

BTS SIO – Exams

The BTS SIO – SISR is evaluated through a combination of continuous assessments and final exams. The exams represent approximately 40 hours of written and practical evaluations, complemented by project presentations.

Detailed Exam Breakdown

  • E4 – Coeff. 5 IT Project Management – 4h written

    Managing IT projects, planning tasks, writing documentation, and proposing solutions according to client needs.

  • E5 – Coeff. 6 Network and Systems Administration – 5h written/practical

    Configuring, maintaining, and troubleshooting IT infrastructures: server admin, network config, security, incident resolution.

  • SISR – Coeff. 4 Cybersecurity & IT Security – 3h written/practical

    Network security, firewalls, user access management, system protection, and vulnerability detection.

  • E6 – Coeff. 3 Technical Support and IT Assistance – 3h practical

    Providing technical support, resolving incidents, and documenting procedures in real-world simulations.

  • Optional – Coeff. 3 Software Development & Scripting – 4h written/practical

    Basic scripting to automate tasks, manage system configurations, and support IT services.

  • E7 – Coeff. 2 Professional Communication & Documentation – 2h written

    Communicate effectively in a professional environment, write reports, and document projects clearly.

  • Internship – Coeff. 6 Internship / Work Placement Evaluation – Continuous assessment

    Evaluated on technical proficiency, professional behavior, and applying theoretical knowledge in real-world situations.
